Colours
Colors are an integral part of our world and perception. They are produced when light is absorbed or reflected by objects and then interpreted by our brains. Colors can evoke emotions and create different moods. For example, red is associated with passion, love, and excitement, while blue is often connected to calmness, serenity, and trustworthiness. Other colors, such as yellow, green, purple, and orange, also have unique meanings and can communicate different messages. Colors are used in a variety of fields, from fashion to marketing, art, and psychology, and play an essential role in human communication and expression.




Primary Colours
Red is a vibrant and intense color that is often associated with passion, love, and energy. It is a primary color that is created by absorbing all colors except red. Red has been used throughout history in various cultures to symbolize different meanings, such as power, courage, and strength. In many Eastern cultures, red is the color of good fortune and happiness. Red is also commonly used in advertising and branding to grab attention and create a sense of urgency. Physiologically, red can stimulate the appetite and increase heart rate, making it a popular choice for restaurants and food products. Overall, red is a powerful color that can convey strong emotions and messages.
Blue is a cool and calming color that is often associated with serenity, stability, and trustworthiness. It is a primary color that is created by absorbing all colors except blue. Blue is a versatile color that can evoke different moods and meanings depending on its shade or context. For example, light blue can convey a sense of tranquility and clarity, while dark blue can represent depth, intelligence, and authority. Blue is commonly used in branding and marketing to establish trust and credibility. It is also a popular color in interior design and fashion, as it can create a soothing and timeless ambiance. Overall, blue is a timeless color that can convey a wide range of emotions and messages.
Yellow is a bright and cheerful color that is often associated with optimism, happiness, and creativity. It is a primary color that is created by absorbing all colors except yellow. Yellow is a versatile color that can evoke different moods and meanings depending on its shade or context. For example, pale yellow can represent innocence and purity, while bright yellow can convey energy and excitement. Yellow is commonly used in branding and marketing to attract attention and convey a sense of fun and playfulness. It is also a popular color in fashion and interior design, as it can create a vibrant and uplifting atmosphere. Overall, yellow is a vibrant and lively color that can bring joy and positivity to any space or message.
